Local Knowledge

What we see in Marshall yards.

Marshall is the Harrison County seat, twenty-five miles east of Longview, and the historic downtown — those grand turn-of-the-century homes around the courthouse — represents a different kind of landscaping work than newer subdivision yards. Mature azaleas, oversized magnolias, decade-old crepe myrtle stands, deep flower beds that have been replanted and remulched a dozen times. These properties need a crew that's comfortable working around established hardscape and roots without tearing things up.

Soil and climate are similar to the rest of our East Texas footprint: sandy loam, acidic, hot humid summers, mild winters. What sets Marshall apart is the volume of older properties with original specimen plantings — the kind of yards where you can't just bring in a stand-on mower and a string trimmer and call it done. We do a lot of fine maintenance work in Marshall: precision hedge shaping, careful bed edging around heritage plants, careful pruning on trees that have been there longer than the owners have.

We're on Marshall routes regularly. Free in-person walks, written estimates, and a crew that's not going to scalp a hundred-year-old boxwood the first time we visit. Call (903) 844-6877 to get on the schedule.
