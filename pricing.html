What Changes Your Price

Why two Longview yards get two different numbers

When you see a range instead of a flat rate, it's because a handful of real things move the total up or down. Here's what we look at when we walk your property:

✓Yard & area size — square footage of turf, beds, or patio is the biggest driver on most jobs.
✓Materials you choose — the type of sod, mulch, pavers, plants, or stone you pick sets a different starting point.
✓Site access — a tight backyard gate or a steep, hard-to-reach spot takes more time than an open, drive-up lot.
✓Plant selection — mature specimens and seasonal color cost more than smaller starter plants.
✓Wall height & linear footage — a taller or longer retaining wall needs more base, block, and engineering than a short garden wall.
✓Site prep & ground conditions — East Texas sandy loam over clay subsoil sometimes needs extra leveling, grading, or drainage before the main work.

None of this is guesswork on our end. We walk the yard, measure what matters, and put every line in writing so you can see exactly what you're paying for. No hidden add-ons — if the ground turns up something unexpected, we show you and get your okay before we keep going.
